Accommodations

Where to stay

Stowe, VT — 4 nights (June 11–15)

Big house with a yard

Look for a 4+ bedroom house in or near Stowe Village. Being walkable to restaurants is the key — Mountain Road or Village area, not up at the resort.

  • 4+ bedrooms, 2+ baths, sleeps 7+
  • Yard for kids to run around
  • Within walking distance of village restaurants
  • Firepit or porch for evening hangs
  • Off-season ski houses = great deals in June
  • Budget: ~$250–400/night for a big house

Montreal — 4 nights (June 15–19)

Large apartment in the Plateau

The Plateau-Mont-Royal or Mile End neighborhoods are ideal — walkable to everything, restaurants on every block, parks nearby. Avoid downtown hotels.

  • 3-4 bedrooms, sleeps 7+
  • Plateau, Mile End, or Outremont neighborhoods
  • Near a park (Parc La Fontaine, Parc Jeanne-Mance)
  • Look for outdoor space — balcony, terrace, small yard
  • Near a metro stop for rainy days
  • Budget: ~$300–500/night (CAD favorable)

The Route

Day by day

Route updates as you scroll through each day
Travel day Wednesday, June 11

White Plains → Stowe (4.5 hrs)

Leave by 8-9am to arrive before the afternoon nap meltdown. The drive is mostly I-87 north through the Hudson Valley, then cuts east into Vermont on Route 89.

  • Stop 1: Saratoga Springs, NY (~2 hrs in) — Stretch legs at Congress Park, grab lunch at Hattie's (Southern comfort food, kid-friendly) or Uncommon Grounds coffee
  • Stop 2: Waterbury, VT (~4 hrs in, 15 min from Stowe) — Cold Hollow Cider Mill for cider donuts. Or save Ben & Jerry's for another day.
  • Arrive Stowe ~1-2pm. Unpack, explore the village, early dinner.
First dinner: The Bench (wood-fired pizza, relaxed, kid-friendly) or Doc Ponds (pub food, great cocktails) — both walkable, no reservation needed
Stowe Thursday, June 12

Settle in — Rec Path + village day

No agenda. Let the kids set the pace. This is your decompression day.

  • Morning: Stowe Recreation Path — grab coffee, put the 2yo in the stroller, let the 4yo scooter. 5.3 miles paved along the West Branch river. Go as far as you want and turn back.
  • Afternoon: Explore the village. Stowe Golf Park (mini golf) or just wander. Find the covered bridge on the Rec Path.
  • If it's warm: Wade in the river at one of the Rec Path access points. The water is cold but the kids won't care.
Dinner: Harrison's — American fare, wood beams, cozy. Walk there and back. Book a table.
Stowe Friday, June 13

Waterfall + swimming holes

  • Morning: Moss Glen Falls (10 min drive) — easy 10-minute walk to a beautiful waterfall. The 4yo can hike it, carry the 2yo. Swimming below the falls if it's warm enough.
  • Midday: Bingham Falls (near Smugglers' Notch) — quarter-mile hike to falls + swimming holes upstream. More adventurous. Optional.
  • Afternoon: Ben & Jerry's factory (15 min in Waterbury). 30-minute tour, ice cream at the end. Hit Cold Hollow Cider Mill next door for donuts if you didn't on Day 1.
Dinner: Hen of the Wood (Waterbury, 15 min) — the best meal of the Stowe leg. Farm-to-table, changes daily. Book ahead. Consider getting a sitter and making this an adults-only dinner.
Stowe Saturday, June 14

Lake Elmore beach day + Smugglers' Notch

  • Morning: Drive to Smugglers' Notch (20 min) — dramatic narrow pass through the mountains. Stop to scramble on the giant boulders. The 4yo will be in heaven. Keep the 2yo close.
  • Afternoon: Lake Elmore State Park (25 min from Stowe) — sandy beach, roped swimming area, lifeguards. Pack a cooler and towels. This is the "beach day" of the trip — calm lake water, warm-ish by mid-June, perfect for toddlers.
  • Evening: Back to Stowe. Last firepit night. Rec Path sunset walk.
Dinner: Plate (small plates, natural wine, intimate) or Doc Ponds if you want something casual. Adults: end the night at Après Cocktails & Wine — hidden inside a day spa, craft cocktails.
Travel day Sunday, June 15

Stowe → Montreal (2.5 hrs)

Easy drive north. No rush — check out by 11, arrive Montreal by 2pm.

  • Route: North on Route 100 to I-89, then I-89 to the border at Highgate Springs / Saint-Armand. Quick border crossing on a Sunday (usually 15-20 min).
  • Optional stop: Burlington (45 min from Stowe) — Church Street pedestrian mall, Lake Champlain waterfront. Worth 60-90 min if the kids need to burn energy before the border.
  • Border tip: Have all 7 passports handy (including kids). Declare any food you're bringing. They'll ask where you're staying and how long.
  • Arrive Montreal ~2-3pm. Settle into the apartment. Walk the neighborhood.
First Montreal meal: Walk to St-Viateur Bagels (Mile End) — watch them come out of the wood-fired oven. Grab a dozen. Then wander to Parc La Fontaine or Parc Jeanne-Mance and let the kids run.
Montreal Monday, June 16

Old Montreal + Old Port

  • Morning: Walk the cobblestones of Old Montreal. Notre-Dame Basilica (stunning interior, the 4yo will be awed by the blue light). Place d'Armes square.
  • Midday: Old Port waterfront — splash pad (free!), carousel, harborside walk. The 2yo will be happy here for hours.
  • Afternoon: Clock Tower Beach area or boat tour of the harbor.
Lunch: Olive et Gourmando (Old Montreal, legendary sandwiches). Dinner: Schwartz's Deli — smoked meat sandwiches since 1928. Cash only, line out the door, worth it. Kids love it (it's basically a diner).
Spa day Tuesday, June 17

Biodome + Bota Bota spa

Split the group. This is the day the adults get their payoff.

  • Morning (everyone): Biodome — four ecosystems under one roof. Penguins, rainforest, lynx, fish. Half-day minimum. The 4yo will be obsessed. The 2yo will love the penguins.
  • Afternoon (adults rotate): 2-3 adults take the kids to Parc Maisonneuve (right next to Biodome, big open green space, playground). The other 2-3 adults go to the spa.
Dinner: Mon Lapin (Little Italy, French-Italian, natural wine) — book ahead. Or Joe Beef if you can get a table (book months ahead, try calling day-of for cancellations).
Montreal Wednesday, June 18

Market + mountain + last night out

  • Morning: Jean-Talon Market — the best outdoor market in North America. Cheese, pastries, fruit, flowers, prepared food. Let the 4yo pick lunch. The 2yo eats samples.
  • Afternoon: Mont-Royal — easy walk or drive to Kondiaronk lookout for panoramic city views. Playground at Parc Jeanne-Mance on the way down. Beaver Lake if kids have energy.
  • Evening: This is the big night. Adults rotate out for the best meal of the trip.
Dinner: Lawrence (Mile End, Michelin Recommended, seasonal) or Mastard (creative, vegetable-forward). After dinner: Cloakroom (no-menu, 25 seats, tell them what you like, they make it) or Atwater Cocktail Club (hidden entrance, world-class).
Fly home Thursday, June 19

Montreal → Home (YUL)

  • Sleep in. Slow morning. Pack up.
  • Last stop: Fairmount Bagels (the other legendary bagel shop, 2 min from St-Viateur) — grab a bag for the plane.
  • YUL airport is 20 min from the Plateau. Major international hub, direct flights to most US cities.
  • Drop the rental car at the airport. Done.
Wellness

The spa

Bota Bota, spa-sur-l'eau

A floating Nordic spa on a restored 1950s ferry in the Old Port of Montreal

Five decks of saunas, steam rooms, outdoor hot tubs, cold plunges, and relaxation areas — all on a boat on the St. Lawrence River with views of Old Montreal. The water circuit alternates hot → cold → rest, repeat. Deeply relaxing. Phones are banned (except Thursdays).

Facilities: 4 saunas with river views, eucalyptus steam bath, outdoor whirlpool baths, cold plunge pools, restaurant on board, garden terrace

When: Tuesday June 17 afternoon — while half the group has the kids at the park after the Biodome. Book 2-3 hours.

Cost: ~$70-90 CAD per person for the water circuit. Massages additional.

Location: Old Port, right on the waterfront. 15 min from the Plateau by metro or taxi.

Alternatives

If Bota Bota is booked or you want more options

Scandinave Spa Old Montreal — Indoor Nordic spa in a historic stone building. Same hot/cold/rest cycle. Enforced silence. More intimate than Bota Bota. ~$80 CAD. scandinave.com

Strom Spa Nordique (Nuns' Island) — Outdoor Nordic spa on an island in the St. Lawrence. 20 min from downtown. Infinity pools, saunas, river views. The most dramatic setting. stromspa.com

Spa at Topnotch (Stowe) — If you'd rather spa during the Stowe leg. 25,000 sq ft, award-winning. More of a resort spa vibe than Nordic. Good for a rainy day in Vermont. topnotchresort.com

En Route

Nice stops along the way

Leg 1: White Plains → Stowe

  • Saratoga Springs, NY (2 hrs) — Good midpoint. Congress Park for the kids, Hattie's for fried chicken, or just a coffee stop at Uncommon Grounds.
  • Manchester, VT (3.5 hrs via Route 7) — If you take the scenic route. Cute town, outlet shopping, Hildene (Lincoln family estate).
  • Waterbury, VT (4 hrs) — 15 min from Stowe. Ben & Jerry's, Cold Hollow Cider Mill, Cabot Cheese. You'll come back here during the trip, so you can skip on arrival day.

Leg 2: Stowe → Montreal

  • Burlington, VT (45 min from Stowe) — Church Street pedestrian mall, Lake Champlain waterfront, Hen of the Wood's second location. Worth a stop if you have time.
  • Shelburne, VT (1 hr from Stowe) — Shelburne Farms (working farm, walking trails, lake views) and Shelburne Museum (art + Americana, outdoor campus).
  • Border crossing at Highgate Springs / Saint-Armand — Usually the quickest crossing on this route. Avoid Champlain/Lacolle on weekends.

Preparation

Logistics

Before you go

  • Valid passports for all 7 (including both kids — check expiry dates now)
  • Book Stowe house (VRBO/Airbnb — June 11-15)
  • Book Montreal apartment (Airbnb — June 15-19)
  • Book Hen of the Wood dinner (Waterbury — June 13)
  • Book Bota Bota spa (June 17 afternoon)
  • Book Montreal dinner (Joe Beef / Mon Lapin / Lawrence — June 17 or 18)
  • Rent car large enough for 7 + luggage (minivan or large SUV)
  • Book flights home from YUL (June 19)
  • Car seats: 2 (bring your own or rent with car)

Packing notes

  • Layers — Stowe mornings are 55°F, afternoons 70°F+
  • Swimsuits + towels for swimming holes and Lake Elmore
  • Water shoes for rocky river bottoms
  • Rain jackets (Vermont weather is unpredictable)
  • Stroller for the 2yo (Rec Path is paved)
  • Scooter or balance bike for the 4yo (Rec Path)
  • Baby carrier/hiking backpack for waterfall hikes
  • Sunscreen + bug spray (early June = blackfly season in VT)
  • CAD cash not strictly needed — tap-to-pay works everywhere in Montreal

Budget rough estimates

  • Stowe house (4 nights): $1,000–1,600
  • Montreal apartment (4 nights): $1,200–2,000 CAD
  • Rental car (9 days): $500–800
  • Gas: ~$120 total
  • Bota Bota spa (2-3 people): $200-270 CAD
  • Groceries: ~$300-400 for the week
  • Dining out: $150-250/day for the group
  • Activities: Most are free or cheap (parks, paths, falls)

Good to know

  • Montreal is French-first. Menus, signs, greetings in French. Everyone speaks English but open with "bonjour."
  • Tipping in Quebec is same as US — 15-20%
  • Quebec uses CAD (~0.73 USD) — your dollar goes further
  • Blackfly season in Vermont peaks late May / early June. Bring DEET. They're annoying near water.
  • Lake Elmore has lifeguards but verify they're staffing by June 14
  • Stowe Rec Path is open year-round but mud can be an issue in early June after rain
  • Montreal Airbnb regulations exist — book licensed properties
