The honest case

Why it works

  • Stowe Rec Path is a daily anchor — 5.3 mi paved, flat, along the river. Stroller + scooter friendly. You'll use it every day.
  • Swimming holes are legit by early June — Moss Glen Falls, Sterling Falls Gorge, ranch camp spots along the river
  • Restaurant scene punches way above its weight — Hen of the Wood, Doc Ponds, Plate, Idletyme, Harrison's
  • Big ski houses sit empty in June = great deals on spacious rentals with yards
  • Ben & Jerry's factory (15 min) — the 4yo will remember this forever
  • Pre-tourist-season: locals are around, crowds aren't, everything's open but not slammed
  • Genuinely beautiful. Green mountains, wildflowers, covered bridges. It photographs well and feels good.
  • Adults can rotate brewery/dinner runs while kids sleep — town is compact

The honest downsides

  • No lake or ocean. River swimming and pools only. If your crew wants beach days, this isn't it.
  • 4.5 hour drive with a 2yo is real. Plan for stops. It's not terrible but it's not nothing.
  • It's a small town. By day 5–6, you'll have eaten at most of the restaurants. Not a huge variety of "new" things to discover.
  • Nightlife is essentially zero. After kids' bedtime it's porch/firepit/board games. (Maybe that's fine.)
  • Early June water is still cold. Swimmable but bracing — not "float lazily" temperature.
  • Rain days with toddlers in a mountain town = limited indoor options beyond the house
  • Burlington (airport) is 45 min away — not a quick Uber-to-terminal situation
  • Cell service can be spotty depending on where the rental is
