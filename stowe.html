The honest case

Why it works

  • Stowe Rec Path is a daily anchor — 5.3 mi paved, flat, along the river. Stroller + scooter friendly.
  • Swimming holes by early June — Moss Glen Falls, Sterling Falls Gorge, ranch camp spots
  • Restaurant scene punches above its weight — Hen of the Wood, Doc Ponds, Plate, Harrison's
  • Big ski houses sit empty in June = great deals on spacious rentals with yards
  • Ben & Jerry's factory 15 min away — the 4yo will remember this forever
  • Pre-tourist-season: locals are around, crowds aren't, everything's open but not slammed
  • Genuinely beautiful. Green mountains, wildflowers, covered bridges.
  • Adults can rotate brewery/dinner runs while kids sleep — town is compact

The honest downsides

  • No lake or ocean. River swimming and pools only. No beach days.
  • 4.5 hour drive with a 2yo. Not terrible but not nothing.
  • Small town. By day 5–6 you've eaten at most restaurants.
  • Nightlife is zero. After bedtime it's porch/firepit/board games.
  • Early June water is still cold. Swimmable but bracing.
  • Rain days with toddlers = limited indoor options beyond the house
  • Burlington airport is 45 min away
  • Cell service can be spotty depending on the rental

The honest case

Why it works

  • The coast is genuinely stunning. Penobscot Bay, harbor towns, lighthouses — it's the postcard.
  • Lobster pounds, clam shacks, the whole Maine food thing. Kids eat lobster mac & cheese, adults eat lobster.
  • Camden Hills State Park — Mt. Battie is a short hike (or drive) with jaw-dropping harbor views
  • Portland day trip (1.5 hrs south) is one of the best food cities in the US per capita
  • Tide pools, rocky beaches, harbor seals — genuinely magical for a 4yo
  • Early June = pre-summer crowds. You'll have the coast mostly to yourselves.
  • More "wow factor" than Vermont — your group will feel like they went somewhere special
  • Good rental houses with ocean views in Camden/Rockport area

The honest downsides

  • The ocean is COLD. Like 52°F cold. Nobody's swimming in the ocean in early June. Full stop.
  • 5.5 hour drive with a 2yo is a hard day. Probably need a real lunch stop midway.
  • Rocky beaches, not sandy. No building sandcastles, no easy wading. It's beautiful but not "beach vacation."
  • Portland airport is 1.5 hrs from Camden/Rockland. That's a real schlep on departure day.
  • Cooler and foggier than Vermont. Early June can be gray and 58°F for days at a stretch.
  • Less walkable than Stowe — you'll drive to most activities. No equivalent of the Rec Path.
  • Restaurants in midcoast towns are good but thin. Not Portland-level outside Portland.
  • Fog + toddlers + rocky coast = you're watching them like a hawk near the water

The honest verdict

Stowe is the easiest, most practical choice. Walkable, great daily rhythm with the Rec Path, excellent food, and off-season ski house rentals are a steal. If your priority is "minimize logistics, maximize chill," Stowe wins.

Lake Placid is the dark horse. Everything Stowe has plus a lake — Mirror Lake is right in town. Olympic history is a bonus. Food is thinner and the airport (Albany, 2 hrs) is worse, but the lake changes the daily rhythm in a big way with kids.

Maine (midcoast or Acadia) is the most memorable. The coast in early June is genuinely special. But you pay for it: longer drive, colder, foggier, less walkable, and the airport logistics are the worst of the bunch.

Montreal is the wildcard. Completely different trip — a world-class city that feels like Europe. Best food of any option by a mile. Unlimited rainy-day plans. The Biodome alone could fill a day with kids. But it's a city vacation, not a nature retreat, and you need passports. If your group wants culture + restaurants + urban energy, this is the one.

Berkshires are closest and most culturally rich, but more "adults with kids in tow." Finger Lakes have the warmest swimming but the main draw (wineries) is wasted. White Mountains have Storyland (genuine kid paradise) but the town is weaker. Thousand Islands are surprisingly magical but better as a long weekend — thin on dining for a full week.

With a 2yo and 4yo for a full week? Stowe or Lake Placid. Walkability and a contained environment matter more than you think when managing two little kids for seven days. Montreal if your group leans urban.
