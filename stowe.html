The honest case

Why it works

  • Stowe Rec Path is a daily anchor — 5.3 mi paved, flat, along the river. Stroller + scooter friendly.
  • Swimming holes by early June — Moss Glen Falls, Sterling Falls Gorge, ranch camp spots
  • Restaurant scene punches above its weight — Hen of the Wood, Doc Ponds, Plate, Harrison's
  • Big ski houses sit empty in June = great deals on spacious rentals with yards
  • Ben & Jerry's factory 15 min away — the 4yo will remember this forever
  • Pre-tourist-season: locals are around, crowds aren't, everything's open but not slammed
  • Genuinely beautiful. Green mountains, wildflowers, covered bridges.
  • Adults can rotate brewery/dinner runs while kids sleep — town is compact

The honest downsides

  • No lake or ocean. River swimming and pools only. No beach days.
  • 4.5 hour drive with a 2yo. Not terrible but not nothing.
  • Small town. By day 5–6 you've eaten at most restaurants.
  • Nightlife is zero. After bedtime it's porch/firepit/board games.
  • Early June water is still cold. Swimmable but bracing.
  • Rain days with toddlers = limited indoor options beyond the house
  • Burlington airport is 45 min away
  • Cell service can be spotty depending on the rental

The honest case

Why it works

  • The coast is genuinely stunning. Penobscot Bay, harbor towns, lighthouses — it's the postcard.
  • Lobster pounds, clam shacks, the whole Maine food thing. Kids eat lobster mac & cheese, adults eat lobster.
  • Camden Hills State Park — Mt. Battie is a short hike (or drive) with jaw-dropping harbor views

  • More "wow factor" than Vermont — your group will feel like they went somewhere special

  • The ocean is COLD. Like 52°F cold. Nobody's swimming in the ocean in early June. Full stop.
  • 5.5 hour drive with a 2yo is a hard day. Probably need a real lunch stop midway.
  • Rocky beaches, not sandy. No building sandcastles, no easy wading. It's beautiful but not "beach vacation."
  • Portland airport is 1.5 hrs from Camden/Rockland. That's a real schlep on departure day.
  • Cooler and foggier than Vermont. Early June can be gray and 58°F for days at a stretch.
  • Less walkable than Stowe — you'll drive to most activities. No equivalent of the Rec Path.
  • Restaurants in midcoast towns are good but thin. Not Portland-level outside Portland.
  • Fog + toddlers + rocky coast = you're watching them like a hawk near the water

The honest case

Why it works

  • It's a national park on the ocean. The wow factor is unmatched. Cadillac Mountain sunrise is a life memory.
  • 45 miles of carriage roads — crushed gravel, car-free, flat to rolling. Stroller + scooter paradise. This is your Rec Path but better.
  • Tide pools everywhere. The 4yo will be obsessed. Harbor seals, starfish, crabs, sea urchins.
  • Bar Harbor village is walkable and charming — ice cream, shops, lobster on the pier
  • Early June is before the summer crush. Park is open, crowds are manageable, weather is warming up.
  • Jordan Pond House — popovers on the lawn overlooking the pond. Iconic. Kids love it.
  • Endless variety for a full week: ocean cliffs, forest trails, lakes, mountains, harbors, islands
  • Sand Beach exists — small but real sand, dramatic cliffs on both sides. Kids can play even if the water's too cold.

The honest downsides

  • 6.5 hours from White Plains. The longest drive. With a 2yo this is a genuinely hard day. Plan an overnight stop or very early departure.
  • The ocean is 50°F. Nobody is swimming. Sand Beach is for looking at, not wading in.
  • Weather is the coldest and foggiest of all options. Early June can be gray and 55°F for days. Pack layers and rain gear.
  • Food in Bar Harbor is lobster + tourist fare. Good, not great. No standout restaurants. You'll eat well but not memorably.
  • Flying home is awkward: BHB is tiny (limited flights, expensive), Portland is 3 hours south.
  • Park reservation system adds a planning layer. Need to book vehicle entry passes in advance for some areas.
  • Cliff edges, rock scrambles, and ocean drop-offs with a 2yo = constant vigilance. Carriage roads are safe, but the dramatic spots are stressful.
  • Rainy days are rough. Bar Harbor has shops but not much else. You're stuck in the rental.

The honest case

Why it works

  • World-class food scene. Joe Beef, Schwartz's deli, St-Viateur bagels, Jean-Talon Market. New restaurant every night for a week and you won't repeat.
  • Feels like Europe without the flight. Cobblestone streets, French signage, cafe culture, patisseries. Your group will feel like they went somewhere genuinely different.
  • The Biodome is a legit half-day with kids — four ecosystems under one roof. The 4yo will be in awe. The 2yo will love the penguins.
  • Unlimited rainy day options. Museums, indoor markets, restaurants, shopping. Weather can't ruin this trip.
  • Extremely walkable. Metro system is excellent. You could park the car and not touch it for a week.
  • Old Montreal is genuinely beautiful — Notre-Dame Basilica, cobblestone squares, harbor area with a splash pad and carousel
  • Lachine Canal path — flat, paved, great for strollers/bikes. Not as scenic as Stowe's Rec Path but solid daily exercise.
  • YUL is a major airport right in the city. Easiest fly-home logistics of any option.

The honest downsides

  • It's a city. If your group wants nature, quiet, and a firepit under the stars, this is the wrong trip entirely.
  • Passports required for all 7 of you including the kids. If anyone's expired, this is a non-starter.
  • No swimming. No lake, no ocean, no river holes. City splash pads and pools only.
  • 5.5 hour drive with border crossing. The border adds 15-60 min depending on the line. With kids and a rental car, have docs ready.
  • Accommodations are hotels or Airbnbs, not big rental houses with yards. Harder to find space for 7 people to spread out.
  • Restaurant dinners with a 2yo in a nice Montreal bistro — doable but stressful. You'll do more takeout/casual than you planned.
  • No "nature escape" feeling. After a week you might feel like you need a vacation from your vacation.
  • Everything is in French first. Not a problem but worth knowing — menus, signs, interactions default to French.

The route

Day 1: White Plains → Stowe (4.5 hrs)

Leave morning, arrive by lunch. Stop in Albany or Rutland for a break. Settle into the house, explore the village, early dinner.

Days 1–4: Stowe — decompress mode

Let the kids set the pace. No agenda pressure.

  • Rec Path every morning — stroller, scooter, coffee
  • Swimming holes in the afternoon when it's warm (Moss Glen, Bingham Falls)
  • Lake Elmore beach day (25 min) — sandy beach, lifeguards, perfect for toddlers
  • Ben & Jerry's factory trip (15 min)
  • Walk to Doc Ponds, The Bench, or Harrison's for dinner
  • Firepit nights after the kids are down
  • Smugglers' Notch boulders for the 4yo
  • Adults rotate a night out at Hen of the Wood (book ahead)

Day 5: Stowe → Montreal (2.5 hrs)

Easy drive north. Cross at Highgate Springs / St-Armand — usually quick on a weekday. Arrive by lunch, grab bagels at St-Viateur, settle into the Airbnb. Walk the neighborhood.

Days 5–8: Montreal — switch gears

Urban energy. New restaurant every night. The adults get their payoff.

  • Day 1: Old Montreal — cobblestones, Notre-Dame, Old Port splash pad + carousel, waterfront walk
  • Day 2: Biodome (half day) + Parc La Fontaine playground + dinner in the Plateau
  • Day 3: Jean-Talon Market morning, Mont-Royal afternoon (Kondiaronk lookout), cocktails at Cloakroom or Atwater
  • Day 4: Lachine Canal bike ride to Atwater Market, or Parc Jean-Drapeau island day
  • Sunday bonus: Tam-tam drum circle on Mont-Royal — free, magical, kids love it
  • Every night: Joe Beef corridor, Schwartz's, Mon Lapin, Lawrence — rotate adults out for the best meals of the trip

Day 8 or 9: Fly home from YUL

Major international airport, 20 min from downtown. Direct flights to most US cities. Drop the rental car, done.

Why this works

The case for the combo

  • Solves Stowe's "too quiet by day 6" problem — you leave before it gets old
  • Solves Montreal's "full week in a city with toddlers" problem — 3-4 days is the sweet spot
  • Two totally different vibes: nature → city. The trip has an arc.
  • The drives are short — 4.5 hrs then 2.5 hrs. Both manageable with a 2yo.
  • YUL is the easiest airport of any option. Major hub, in the city.
  • Best food of any itinerary — Hen of the Wood + Joe Beef + Schwartz's + Mon Lapin in one trip
  • Routing is perfectly linear: south → north → fly home. No backtracking.
  • Adults get porch/firepit nights AND world-class cocktail bars. Both halves deliver.

The honest downsides

  • Two accommodations = two check-ins, two pack/unpacks. With toddlers, moving day is a lost day.
  • Stowe ski house deals are better for full weeks — 4 nights may cost nearly as much as 7.
  • Montreal Airbnb for 7 people with enough space is harder to find than a big Vermont house.
  • Border crossing adds 15–60 min of uncertainty. Have all 7 passports ready (including kids).
  • More planning than a single-destination trip. Two sets of restaurants to research, two areas to learn.
  • If it rains the whole Stowe leg, you might wish you'd just done Montreal for the full week.
  • Currency switch — minor but you'll want CAD/tap-to-pay ready for Montreal.
  • The 2yo won't remember any of this. You're really doing this for the adults and the 4yo.

The honest verdict

The combo is the move: Stowe (4 days) → Montreal (3–4 days). You get nature + city, porch firepits + world-class cocktail bars, swimming holes + the Biodome. The drives are short, the routing is linear, and you fly home from a major airport. It solves every weakness of doing either place alone.

If one destination only? Stowe for pure ease. Walkable, contained, great daily rhythm with the Rec Path, and off-season ski house rentals are a steal. Add Lake Elmore (25 min) for beach days. You might get antsy by day 6 but you won't be stressed.

Maine (midcoast or Acadia) is the most dramatic and memorable, but the logistics tax — longer drives, fog, cold water, awkward airports — is real with two toddlers. Save it for when the youngest is 5+.

Montreal alone for a full week would be incredible for the adults but 7 days in a city with a 2yo is a lot. 3–4 days is the sweet spot — which is exactly what the combo gives you.

Decision tree: Passports valid for all 7? → Stowe + Montreal combo. No passports? → Stowe for the week, add Lake Elmore and a Burlington day trip.
